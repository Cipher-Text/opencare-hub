Product Vision

A trusted, modular healthcare information & service platform for Bangladesh and South Asia, built with privacy-first principles, RBAC, and audit trails.

Technical Docs Trust & Safety

Executive Summary

Open Care unifies patients, doctors, nurses, hospitals, diagnostics, and social organizations to reduce fragmentation, improve verified access, and modernize provider operations.

Problem Statement

Trust Gaps

Patients struggle to verify providers and service quality.

Low Visibility

Providers lack standardized digital profiles and workflows.

Home Care Shortage

Verified, structured nursing services remain limited.

Financial Strain

High out-of-pocket costs reduce care access.

Fragmented Ecosystem

Stakeholders operate in silos with minimal coordination.

Target Users

Patients

Verified access, appointments, records, fundraising.

Doctors

Visibility, scheduling, verified reputation, content.

Nurses

Verified profiles, home-care scheduling, documentation.

Hospitals & Clinics

Institution profiles, staff listing, service routing.

Diagnostic Centers

Test listings, referrals, digital reports.

Organizations & Associations

Events, campaigns, volunteer programs.

Vision Pillars

  • Access: trusted discovery and service pathways
  • Trust: verified providers, RBAC, audit trails
  • Affordability: transparent care + fundraising
  • Efficiency: modern tools for provider operations
  • Community: associations, volunteers, campaigns

Privacy-first by Design

Minimal data collection with role-based access and audit trails for sensitive actions.

Key Differentiators

Nurse-Centric Home Care

Structured scheduling, verification, and service documentation.

Verified Network

Credential checks and audit trails across listings and campaigns.

Role-Based Dashboards

Tailored workflows for each stakeholder role.

Fundraising Transparency

Verification and clear reporting to build trust.

Integrated Services

Appointments, diagnostics, reports, and communication.

Community Engagement

Associations, volunteers, and educational content.

Strategic Roadmap

Phase 1

Verified directory and advanced search.

Phase 2

Role-based dashboards, appointments, home care.

Phase 3

Verification depth and fundraising transparency.

Phase 4

Health records, prescriptions, diagnostics reporting.

Phase 5

Monetization systems and partner services.

Phase 6

Telehealth rollout and community expansion.

Success Metrics (Illustrative)

  • User growth across patients and providers
  • Appointments and services utilization
  • Verified profiles coverage and low abuse rates
  • Rural access and continuity-of-care signals
  • Diversified revenue performance

Risk & Mitigation

  • Regulatory shifts → ongoing compliance monitoring
  • Data security → privacy-first, RBAC, audit trails
  • Competition → nurse-centric differentiation
  • Adoption pace → phased GTM and partnerships
